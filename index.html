✦ STEP BY STEP ✦
The Experiment 🌸

Click each step card to expand the juicy details ✨

✦ STEP 01 ✦

💧 Sodium Oleate Solutions

Prepare four serial dilutions (0.01 M → 1×10⁻⁵ M) of sodium oleate in distilled water. Allow foam to settle before making up to the mark.

🌸 Serial dilution minimises accumulated weighing error. Read the meniscus at the base of the foam — not the fluffy top! 🫧

✦ STEP 02 ✦

⚗️ Krüss Tensiometer

The platinum Wilhelmy plate is lowered to contact the liquid. Wetting force is recorded by the microbalance. γ = F / (l · cosθ_plate).

✨ Wilhelmy equation: F = γ · P · cosθ_plate. For clean platinum θ_plate ≈ 0° so F = γ·P. The Krüss K12 firmware computes γ automatically — love a girl who does the work for you 💁‍♀️

✦ STEP 03 ✦

🌾 Powder Preparation

Crush each powder (mortar & pestle) to remove aggregates. Pack ~1/3 vial volume onto a filter paper base with consistent tamping force.

💖 Consistent packing = consistent C. C is SO sensitive to packing variation — treat your powder bed like your skincare routine: gentle and reproducible!

✦ STEP 04 ✦

🧴 Hexane Reference — Find C

Immerse inverted vial in n-hexane (γ=17.9 mN/m) for exactly 60 s. Since θ=0° for hexane, cosθ=1 and C can be solved directly.

m² = [C · ρ² · γ · cosθ / η] · t

With cosθ = 1: C = m²·η / (ρ²·γ·t) — this material constant is your powder's personality! 💅

✦ STEP 05 ✦

📐 Contact Angle Calculation

Repeat absorption with each surfactant solution. With C known, solve for cosθ then θ = arccos(cosθ). Four concentrations → four contact angles per powder!

cos θ = m² · η / (C · ρ² · γ · t)

🌸 Four sodium oleate concentrations → four γ values → four contact angles per powder. Plot them against γ and you get the gorgeous Zisman plot! 📈✨

✦ STEP 06 ✦

📊 Zisman Plot & γ꜀

Plot θ (y) vs. γ (x). Extrapolate the linear fit to θ = 0° — the x-intercept is the critical surface tension γ꜀ of your powder!

💜 Why is γ꜀ non-physical?

A negative γ꜀ (P1) and a value exceeding mercury's surface tension ~485 mN/m (P2) both indicate the data doesn't have the required monotonic trend for a valid Zisman extrapolation. The tested γ range was too narrow (25–49 mN/m) and confined to one liquid family (surfactant-water). The Owens–Wendt probe set would fix this! 🔬

✦ LET'S TALK ABOUT IT ✦
Discussion 💬🌸

💖 Finding 1 — Why Are All Contact Angles So High? (71°–85°)

Both powders show contact angles far exceeding theoretical predictions. Literature predicts θ ≈ 50–60° for native starch and θ ≈ 20–40° for uncoated TiO₂/ZnO with surfactant solutions.

The most likely culprit is surface hydrophobisation. Cosmetic-grade TiO₂ and ZnO are routinely coated with dimethicone, stearic acid, or alumina — Evonik Aeroxide T 805 gives θ ≈ 95–130° with water! For flour, lipid and protein surface layers on starch granules increase hydrophobicity vs. pure starch (Holmberg et al., 2003).

✨ Finding 2 — Why Did the Zisman Extrapolation Fail?

A valid Zisman analysis requires: (a) monotonic decrease in cosθ as γ increases; (b) a linear trend; (c) data that brackets cosθ = 1. All three failed here.

The γ range tested was narrow (25–49 mN/m) and confined to one liquid family. The Owens–Wendt probe set — n-hexane (17.9), diiodomethane (50.8), formamide (57.0), water (72.8 mN/m) — would independently probe dispersive and polar surface energy components for a valid extrapolation (Owens & Wendt, 1969).

🌸 Finding 3 — Material Constant C & Packing Error

C encodes powder bed porosity and pore tortuosity. The error in cosθ due to C variation scales as Δcosθ ∝ ΔC/(C·sinθ) — which diverges as θ → 90°, exactly where our data sits. For Powder 2 (TiO₂/ZnO), denser particle packing is harder to reproduce manually, explaining the non-monotonic scatter. A calibrated compression ram would fix this!

💚 Finding 4 — Dynamic Adsorption of Sodium Oleate

Sodium oleate adsorbs onto TiO₂ and ZnO surfaces via carboxylate–metal coordination within seconds. The θ measured at t = 60 s is an average over an evolving adsorption process, not a true equilibrium value. This dynamic effect is most pronounced for P2 at high concentrations (0.01 M) where adsorption is fastest — contributing to the non-monotonic scatter we see.

💜 Finding 5 — Cosmetic Formulation Relevance (This Is Our World! 💅)

In a colour-changing foundation system (e.g., Tagra CameleonCaps™ in an O/W base), pigment wettability governs dispersion stability and colour development. The finding that θ > 70° for TiO₂/ZnO in aqueous media confirms that sodium oleate alone is insufficient as a wetting agent for cosmetic-grade inorganic pigments.

For an O/W emulsion, a mixed system of polysorbate 60 (steric stabilisation) + sodium lauryl sulfate (electrostatic stabilisation) would provide superior wettability while maintaining Tagra shell integrity below 40°C. Science meets skincare! ✨🌸
